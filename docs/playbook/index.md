## Two Playbook Views

This site offers **two complementary cuts** of practical architecture guidance — pick the one matching your task:

| View | Organised by | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Playbook (this section)** | *Engagement phase* — Discovery → Design → Validation → Implementation → Operate | You're running an engagement end-to-end |
| [**Domain Playbooks**](../../playbooks.html) (visual page) | *Architecture domain* — Business, Application, Data, Integration, Security, Technology | You're designing within one domain |

The two views overlap on purpose. The phase-view tells you *what to do this week*; the domain-view tells you *what good design looks like in your area of responsibility*.

---

## Engagement vs. ADM

The **5-phase engagement model** in this playbook is *not* the TOGAF ADM. It is a **practical scaffolding for one engagement**, designed to map cleanly onto agile/iterative delivery.

```
Engagement phase    Maps roughly to TOGAF ADM
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Discovery     →  Preliminary, A (Vision), B (Business)
2. Design        →  C (Information Systems), D (Technology)
3. Validation    →  E (Opportunities & Solutions), pre-go-live reviews
4. Implementation→  F (Migration Planning), G (Implementation Governance)
5. Operate       →  H (Change Management), in-life governance
```

When you need to know *what good looks like* for a TOGAF artefact, click into the [Knowledge Library](../knowledge/index.md). Each Playbook page links to the relevant Knowledge page so you can drill in for depth.

## Convention

Pages in this Playbook are **short, scannable, and action-oriented**. They tell you *what to do, in what order, with what artefacts*. Theory and trade-offs live in the Knowledge Library — Playbook pages link to Knowledge for context, then move on.
