## Available Templates

| Template | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **ADR (Architecture Decision Record)** | Capturing a significant decision and its rationale — see also the [ADR template in the ADR index](../../knowledge/decision-records/index.md#adr-template) |
| **RFC (Request for Comments)** | Proposing a change that affects multiple teams; needs review before implementation |
| **Architecture Review Checklist** | Pre-implementation review of a significant design |
| **Project / Solution Brief** | One-page summary of context, decision and outcome for stakeholders |

---

## Template Principles

A good template:

1. **Asks the right questions** — fields force you to confront the hard parts (consequences, alternatives, owner)
2. **Is one screen long** — anything longer is a wizard, not a template
3. **Has examples baked in** — placeholder text shows the level of detail expected
4. **Is versioned** — when the template changes, the change is recorded
