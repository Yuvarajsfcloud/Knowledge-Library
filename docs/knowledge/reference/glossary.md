# Glossary

> Common architecture terms with TOGAF-aligned definitions. Where TOGAF defines a term formally, the definition tracks the TOGAF Standard 10ed wording.

---

## A

**ABB — Architecture Building Block.** A capability-defining building block that describes *what* fundamental functionality is required, not *how* it is implemented. Implemented by one or more SBBs. See [Building Blocks](building-blocks.md).

**ADM — Architecture Development Method.** The iterative process at the heart of TOGAF: Preliminary, A (Vision), B (Business), C (Information Systems), D (Technology), E (Opportunities & Solutions), F (Migration Planning), G (Implementation Governance), H (Change Management).

**ADR — Architecture Decision Record.** Immutable record of a significant architecture decision. Once Accepted, supersede with a new ADR; never edit. See [Decision Records](../decision-records/index.md).

**Architecture Contract.** Formal agreement between architecture and delivery, capturing the architectural constraints delivery must meet. Produced in Phase F; enforced in Phase G.

**Architecture Definition Document (ADD).** The principal output of Phases B, C, and D — the description of the target architecture across business, data, application, and technology domains.

**Architecture Repository.** The structured store of architecture-related content: standards (Reference Library), reusable solutions (Solutions Library), and current landscape. See [Architecture Repository](architecture-repository.md).

## B

**Baseline Architecture.** The current state — the architecture the enterprise has today.

**Building Block.** A package of functionality defined to meet business needs. Two types: ABB and SBB.

## C

**Capability.** A particular ability that a business possesses or exchanges to achieve a specific purpose. Captured in Phase B.

**Compliance Review.** Phase G activity — formal review of an in-flight or in-life implementation against the Architecture Contract. See [Implementation Governance](../adm/implementation-governance.md).

**Continuum.** See Enterprise Continuum.

## D

**Dispensation.** Formally approved deviation from an architecture standard. See [Implementation Governance](../adm/implementation-governance.md).

## E

**Enterprise Continuum.** Classification scheme for architecture content: Foundation, Common Systems, Industry, Organisation-Specific. See [Enterprise Continuum](enterprise-continuum.md).

**Engagement.** A bounded piece of architecture work — typically one project or programme. The Track-B [Engagement Playbook](../../playbook/engagement/index.md) gives a 5-phase practical model for running engagements.

## G

**Gap Analysis.** Comparison of baseline and target architecture per domain, identifying what is missing, redundant, or different. Performed in Phases B, C, and D.

## P

**Phase A–H, Preliminary.** TOGAF ADM phases. See [ADM index](../adm/index.md).

**Principle.** A statement of intent that guides architecture decisions. Captured in the Preliminary Phase. Format: Statement, Rationale, Implications.

## S

**SBB — Solution Building Block.** A building block that implements one or more ABBs using specific products, technologies, or services. See [Building Blocks](building-blocks.md).

**Stakeholder.** Any person or group with an interest in the architecture or the system being delivered.

## T

**Target Architecture.** The future state the enterprise is moving towards.

**Transition Architecture.** A coherent intermediate state between baseline and target — produced in Phase E. The enterprise can pause and operate stably at each Transition Architecture.

## V

**Variance.** A documented deviation between as-built and the documented architecture. Tracked in the Variance Log; created when a Dispensation is granted.

**Viewpoint.** A specification of the conventions for constructing and using a view. ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 term.

**View.** A representation of the architecture from the perspective of a specific stakeholder concern. ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 term. See [Views & Viewpoints](views-viewpoints.md).

## W

**Work Package.** A unit of delivery — typically 3–6 months, fully scoped, single owner. Produced in Phase E.
